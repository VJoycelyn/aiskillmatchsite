Module 1: Foundations of Artificial Intelligence

This module introduces Artificial Intelligence (AI), its historical development, types, and real-world relevance—especially in Caribbean innovation sectors.

What is Artificial Intelligence?

AI refers to systems or machines that mimic human intelligence to perform tasks and can iteratively improve themselves based on the information they collect. These systems can include voice recognition, decision-making, translation, and more.

A Brief History of AI

  • 1950s: Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test.
  • 1956: AI coined at the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1970s–1980s: Expert systems and symbolic AI dominate.
  • 2000s–Present: Machine learning and deep learning expand due to big data and better computing power.

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: Performs a specific task (e.g., virtual assistants).
  • General AI: Hypothetical human-like cognitive AI.
  • Superintelligent AI: Theoretical future AI that surpasses human intelligence.

Applications of AI

AI is already transforming industries:

  • Chatbots for customer service
  • Fraud detection in finance
  • Self-driving vehicles
  • Recommendation engines (e.g., Netflix, YouTube)
  • In the Caribbean: climate monitoring, smart tourism, and disease detection
